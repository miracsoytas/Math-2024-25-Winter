## 3. For \(f(x) = \cos(x)\), verify that \(f''(x) = -f(x)\).


## 2. Proofs

### **Prove \( \frac{d}{dx}(\ln(\sin(x))) = \cot(x) \):**

- **Solution**: Use the chain rule \( \frac{d}{dx}\ln(u) = \frac{1}{u}u' \):
  - \( u = \sin(x), u' = \cos(x) \),
  - \( \frac{d}{dx}(\ln(\sin(x))) = \frac{1}{\sin(x)} \cdot \cos(x) = \cot(x) \).
=======
```math
f'(x) = -\sin(x)
```
